## The Foundation: High-Quality Pellets

African Greys do best on a diet that's 60–70% high-quality pellets (Harrison's, Roudybush, or Zupreem Natural). Pellets provide balanced nutrition that seeds alone cannot.

## Fresh Produce (20–30% of diet)

Rotate these daily:
- **Leafy greens:** kale, collards, Swiss chard, romaine
- **Vegetables:** sweet potato (cooked), broccoli, bell peppers, carrots
- **Fruits (limit):** apple, pomegranate, blueberries, mango — no more than 10% total diet

## Seeds: Treats, Not Staples

Sunflower seeds and safflower seeds are high-fat. Use as training rewards or occasional treats — not as a meal base.

## Critical Nutrients for African Greys

**Calcium** — African Greys are prone to hypocalcemia. Offer calcium-rich greens daily and talk to your avian vet about supplementation.

**Vitamin A** — Deficiency is common. Sweet potato, dark leafy greens, and carrots are excellent natural sources.

## Foods to Avoid

- Avocado (toxic)
- Chocolate and caffeine (toxic)
- Onions and garlic (toxic in large amounts)
- Alcohol (toxic)
- High-sodium processed foods

## Water

Fresh filtered water daily. Stainless steel or ceramic bowls are easier to keep bacteria-free than plastic.
