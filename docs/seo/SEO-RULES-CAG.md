**6\. Non-Commodity’ Content Agent \- I think we did this already, how do i use it when creating content** 

To create a Non-Commodity Content Agent, you have to move away from "Instruction-based" prompts (e.g., "Write a blog about Maltipoos") and move toward a "Heuristic-based" System.

A Non-Commodity Agent doesn’t just write; it mines for original insights that a generic LLM wouldn't know. Here is how to structure it using a Multi-Agent "Insight-First" Architecture.

---

1\. The Core Architecture (The "Triad" Model)

Instead of one agent, you use three specialized roles that "argue" with each other to ensure the output isn't generic.

* The Archaeologist (Research & Discovery): Its skill is to ignore the first 10 pages of Google. It looks for "Friction Points"—unsolved problems, controversial reddit threads, or specific breeder logs.  
* The Provocateur (Insight Generation): Its skill is "The Counter-Narrative." If the internet says "Maltipoos are easy," this agent must find the one reason they are difficult but rewarding.  
* The Stylist (Voice & Tone): Its skill is "Narrative Weaving." It takes the raw insights and wraps them in the specific humor we discussed (like the "Puppy CEO" or "The Honesty Policy").

2\. "The Honesty Policy" (Relatable Parenting Humor)

Maltipoos are adorable, but puppies are also chaotic. Using humor to acknowledge the "messy" side of pet ownership builds massive trust.

* How it works: Use self-deprecating humor about the life of a breeder or the reality of a new puppy.  
* Example: "Our Maltipoos are bred for intelligence, beauty, and the uncanny ability to find the one expensive shoe you left out in under four seconds."  
* Why it works: It shows you are an experienced, "real" breeder rather than a "puppy mill" or a sterile corporate entity.

3\. "The Interviewer" Tone (The Reverse Vet-Check)

Turn the application process into a humorous "job interview" for the potential owners.

* How it works: Frame the puppy application as if the puppy is hiring the owner.  
* Example: "Are you prepared for a 2:00 AM performance of 'The Song of My People' (a solo bark-era)? Do you have a high tolerance for fluff-covered black leggings? Apply to be Barnaby's forever-intern today."  
* Why it works: It makes your "vetting" process feel less like an interrogation and more like a fun community you're inviting them into.

4\. Punny & Playful Branding (The Wordplay approach)

Maltipoos are a "designer crossbreed," which is ripe for wordplay.

* How it works: Lean into the "Poo" in Maltipoo or the "Malti" (Multi) aspect.  
* Example: "Life is better with a Malti-tude." or "Our puppies are 50% Maltese, 50% Poodle, and 100% likely to steal your spot on the couch."  
* Why it works: Short, punchy humor is perfect for social media captions and headlines that need to be "scroll-stopping."

5\. "The Comparison" Humor (Low-Stakes Absurdism)

Compare the puppies to things that aren't dogs to highlight their traits.

* How it works: Use analogies that highlight how small, soft, or smart they are.  
* Example: "Technically, this is a dog. Functionally, it is a sentient marshmallow that has learned how to operate a squeaky toy."  
* Why it works: It creates a vivid, funny image in the buyer's mind that sticks with them more than a list of "hypoallergenic, 5lbs, white coat."
