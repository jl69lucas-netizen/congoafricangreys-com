**Congo African Grey Parrot – Psittacus erithacus**

The Congo African Grey Parrot, scientifically known as [**Psittacus** **erithacus**](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Grey_parrot "Learn more about African Grey on Wikipedia"), is a marvel of the avian world.Predominantly grey with striking red tail feathers, these parrots are celebrated for their exceptional cognitive abilities and emotional depth.In captivity, with proper care, they can live between 40 to 60 years, making them lifelong companions.

However, their wild counterparts face challenges, with average lifespans of around 23 years due to environmental threats.Recognizing their endangered status, **[CITES](https://cites.org/eng/node/48684 "CITES Permit -African Grey Parrot")** has listed them under Appendix I, highlighting the importance of conservation efforts. ​

## **What to Know When Getting an African Grey?**

Embarking on the journey of African Grey companionship requires a commitment to their complex needs:

- **Lifespan Commitment**: [**With lifespans reaching up to 60 years in captivity**](https://birdfact.com/articles/how-long-do-african-greys-live? "African Grey Parrot Lifespan - Birdfact"), adopting an African Grey is a long-term commitment, often spanning generations.

- **Intellectual Engagement**: Renowned for their intelligence, these parrots require constant mental stimulation.Interactive toys, puzzles, and regular social interaction are vital to prevent boredom and associated behavioral issues.​

- **Dietary Needs**: A balanced diet is crucial.High-quality pellets should comprise 50-70% of their intake, complemented by fresh fruits, vegetables, grains, and legumes.Nuts and seeds can be offered sparingly as treats. ​

- **Space and Exercise**: A spacious cage, ideally measuring 36” x 24” x 48”, allows for free movement.Daily out-of-cage time is essential for physical health and psychological well-being.​

- **Social Interaction**: [**African Greys thrive on social bonds**](https://www.thesprucepets.com/african-grey-parrots-390502 "Caring for an African Grey Parrot").Regular interaction with their human companions fosters trust and reduces feelings of isolation.​

### **📌 Do African Greys Like to Cuddle?**

✔ Our African Greys are known for their affectionate nature, but their expression of love differs from traditional mammalian cuddling.While some may enjoy gentle head scratches, many are not fond of full-body handling.Over-cuddling can inadvertently stimulate hormonal behaviors, leading to potential frustration. Observing and respecting your parrot’s comfort levels is essential, ensuring interactions remain positive and stress-free.​

### **📌 Do African Grey Parrots Like Being Touched?**

✔ Touch preferences vary among individual African Greys.Generally, they appreciate gentle scratches around the head and neck regions.However, touching other areas, such as the back or under the wings, can be misinterpreted as mating behaviors, potentially leading to hormonal imbalances and behavioral issues.Always approach with sensitivity, allowing your parrot to guide the interaction.​

### **📌 How to Tell if an African Grey Parrot Likes You?**

✔ Building a bond with an African Grey is a rewarding experience. Signs of affection and trust include:

- **Vocal** **Mimicry**: Imitating your speech or sounds indicates a desire to communicate and bond.​

- **Relaxed Posture**: A calm demeanor, with fluffed feathers and a comfortable stance, signifies comfort in your presence.​

- **Beak Grinding**: Often occurring before sleep, this gentle grinding sound indicates contentment.​

- **Seeking Interaction**: Approaching you, making eye contact, and initiating play are clear signs of affection.​

- **Feather Preening**: Preening in your presence or attempting to present you is a gesture of trust and bonding.​


### **📌 Do African Greys Like to Be Held?**

✔ While African Greys form deep bonds with their human companions, they often prefer proximity without extensive handling.Perching on your arm or shoulder allows them to be close without the confinement of being held. Recognizing and respecting their boundaries is crucial, ensuring interactions are always on their terms.​
