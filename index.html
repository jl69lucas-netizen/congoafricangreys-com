Health & Common Conditions of African Grey Parrots

At C.A.Gs, the health of our parrots is our top priority. Our Congo African Grey parrots undergo comprehensive health screenings before being made available for sale, ensuring they are in excellent condition.

Common African Grey Health Issues

  • Feather Plucking is often a result of boredom or stress, which can be minimized through proper stimulation and enrichment.
  • Psittacosis (Parrot Fever) is a bacterial infection; all our birds are rigorously tested to prevent this condition.
  • Calcium Deficiency: Managed through a balanced diet supplemented with calcium to ensure strong bones and feathers.
  • Respiratory Infections: Preventable with a clean, well-ventilated environment and regular health check-ups.

How to Care for Your African Grey Parrot

Proper care is essential to ensure your African Grey’s healthy, happy life. Our comprehensive care instructions cover everything from nutrition to socialization and training.

African Grey Diet & Nutrition

✔️ Balanced Diet: A mix of fresh fruits, vegetables, nuts, and high-quality pellets provides the necessary nutrients for your parrot.
✔️ Foods to Avoid: Chocolate, caffeine, avocados, and salty foods are toxic and should be strictly avoided.
✔️ Calcium Supplements: Recommended to prevent deficiencies and support strong bones and feathers.

❓How can I tell if my African Grey is male or female?

✔️ African Grey parrots do not have visible gender differences, but you can determine their sex through:

 DNA Testing – The most accurate method; requires a feather or blood sample.
 Physical Traits – Some slight variations:
Males – Larger body, flatter head, darker eye ring.
Females – Smaller, rounder head, lighter eye ring.
