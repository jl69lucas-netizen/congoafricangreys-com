## 🚨 P0 FACT-CHECK FINDING — CITES STATUS IS WRONG IN PROJECT DOCS

The World Parrot Trust encyclopedia (parrots.org) states the Grey Parrot is:
- **CITES Appendix I** (uplisted from Appendix II at CoP17, effective Jan 2017) — *not* "Appendix II"
- **IUCN status: Endangered** (Congo *Psittacus erithacus*); Timneh *Psittacus timneh* = Vulnerable

**Project docs (CLAUDE.md, grill-me skill) currently say "CITES Appendix II" — this is outdated/incorrect.** Publishing "Appendix II" on the homepage is a factual error that scam-wary buyers and AI engines can catch → damages EEAT and trust.

**Action:** Do NOT write CITES claims until the breeder confirms framing. Correct, trust-building framing for a captive-bred breeder:
> "African Greys are protected under **CITES Appendix I**. Every bird we place is **captive-bred in the United States** with full documentation — never wild-caught. Captive-bred Appendix-I birds are legal to own and transfer domestically with proper paperwork."

This actually *strengthens* CAG's positioning (Appendix I = rarer/more protected = captive-bred sourcing matters more). **Flagged for breeder sign-off at the outline gate.**

> **BREEDER CONFIRMED (2026-05-29):** "They were moved from Appendix II up to Appendix I (the highest level of protection) to restrict commercial trade and save wild populations from rampant poaching and habitat loss." → Use this exact framing in the History, CITES, and scam-trust sections. Approved framing: **Appendix I + captive-bred-in-USA, never wild-caught.**
