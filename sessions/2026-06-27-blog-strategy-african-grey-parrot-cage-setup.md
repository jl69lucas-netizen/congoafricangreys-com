### A. Framework
1. **PAS + EBP hybrid (Recommended)** — Problem→Agitate→Solve woven with Evidence→Benefit→Proof. **Why:** the SERP is won on a *safety/fear* axis (zinc death, plucking, head entrapment) that rivals state flatly; PAS surfaces the stakes and EBP backs every claim with breeder proof — exactly the E-E-A-T gap. **Trade-off:** PAS can read alarmist → gate with calm first-person breeder voice, ≤1 fear beat per section.
2. QAB — clean Q→A→Benefit; safest for snippets but **doesn't dramatize the safety wedge** that differentiates us.
3. BAB — Before→After→Bridge; great for the enrichment section but too soft as the page-wide spine.

### B. Winning Angle
1. **"The cage standard we send every C.A.Gs chick home to" (Recommended)** — pure breeder-experience moat no competitor can copy; ties care → purchase. **Trade-off:** must stay inside the Verified-Claim Ledger (don't overclaim aviary specifics).
2. "5 cheap-cage mistakes that cause feather plucking" — strong fear hook, high CTR; **trade-off:** more negative tone, leans on health claims.
3. "Congo vs Timneh: how their cages differ" — owns an unserved sub-niche; **trade-off:** narrower, better as a section than the whole page.

### C. Primary Keyword
1. **`african grey parrot cage setup` as primary + `african grey cage size` as H2 head-term (Recommended)** — captures the broad build-out intent (our slug) *and* the highest-volume specific query rivals rank for. **Trade-off:** two foci → must keep density natural (1.5–2%).
2. `african grey cage size` as primary — higher exact volume but **narrows the page to dimensions**, surrenders perches/placement/enrichment intent.

### D. Mid-Article Special-Element (MIDDLE slot)
1. **Decision tree "Which cage size & spacing for your Grey?" (Recommended)** — Congo/Timneh × budget → recommendation; AI-extraction-friendly, uses our `cag-blog-decision-tree` component, converts. **Trade-off:** more build effort than a static card.
2. Myth-vs-Fact ("round cages are fine" / "3/4\" is always safest") — fast, but lower conversion.
3. Inline available-birds soft-CTA — highest commercial pull, **but** thin on the info intent that dominates this query.
