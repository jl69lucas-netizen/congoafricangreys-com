**Higher taxonomy:** Psittaciformes → Psittacidae. Greys = **Old World** (Africa); Amazons = **New World** (Mexico, Central & South America, Caribbean). *This Old-World/New-World split is the single cleanest structural hook on the page and no competitor uses it.*

**Genus-vs-species asymmetry — the page's core structural insight:** "African Grey" names two closely related birds; "Amazon parrot" names thirty. Comparing them is not symmetric. Every competitor treats "Amazon" as one bird. **birdgap is the only page that even mentions "31 subspecies"** (and it miscalls species "subspecies").

## 4. Health & veterinary entities

**Shared:** avian veterinarian · quarantine · health certificate · psittacosis (*Chlamydia psittaci*) · Vitamin A deficiency · Avian Polyomavirus (APV) · PBFD (*Psittacine Beak and Feather Disease*)

**Grey-leaning:** hypocalcaemia / calcium deficiency · Vitamin D3 & UV-B synthesis · feather-destructive behaviour · respiratory sensitivity · **Bird Fancier's Lung / hypersensitivity pneumonitis** (the human risk from powder down — *birdaddicts is the only competitor that names it*) ⚠ CITE to a medical source, not to birdaddicts

**Amazon-leaning:** obesity · **hepatic lipidosis / fatty liver disease** (PetHelpful names it) · hormonal aggression · chlamydiosis (birdgap)

**PetHelpful also claims** Greys are "prone to heart conditions and seizures, particularly in their old age" ⚠ CITE — plausible (atherosclerosis is documented in psittacines) but do not repeat on a single blog's authority.

### ❌ Claims we must NOT repeat
- **"Immunized: Yes / Vaccination: Up-to-date"** (talkingparrotsisland bird cards). There is no routine core vaccination protocol for companion psittacines. Repeating this would be a trust-destroying falsehood.
- **"a professional avian behaviorist said…"** (vetexplainspets ×3, all unnamed). We cite named people or nobody.
- **"only a few generations removed from the wild, if not wild-caught"** (PetHelpful). We rebut this, we don't echo it.
- **Any decibel figure** unless sourced. exoticpethub's "Soft murmurs: 50-70 dB / Loud calling: 90-100 dB" appears invented.
- **Any weight figure from birdgap** ("roughly 2.5 pounds" ≈ 1,134 g — wrong by ~2×) or exoticpethub ("250–500 grams" for a Congo).
