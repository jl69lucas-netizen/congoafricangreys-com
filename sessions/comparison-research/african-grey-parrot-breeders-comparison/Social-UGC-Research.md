## 3. Parrot Forums — "How to identify a parrot scam" (full content captured)
Real, quotable heuristics from the thread (paraphrase in our voice, attribute the *idea* not the user):
- **Reverse-image-search the photo** — *"A scammer doesn't have the parrot… they scoop a photo from Google. Right-click → Search Google for this image."*
- **Paste the ad text into search** — *"Copy a chunk of the text… you might find the original post it was stolen from."*
- **Too-good-to-be-true pricing** — *"a hyacinth macaw is NOT going to be for sale for $900, not for any reason."*
- **Vague / payment-first behavior** — *"Scammers will be vague, indirect and often times will just want to get right to the payment part."*
- **Fake-ad tells** — *"parrots are never spayed and neutered. If the ad includes that, it's a scam. Same for 'up to date on shots.'"* · *"if it's parrot eggs, it's a scam."*
- **Area-code mismatch** — *"a telephone area code that doesn't match the advertised location… a good indication it might be."*
- **The affirmative counter (rare, valuable):** *"The decent, honest people out there (they ARE out there!) want their birds to go to good homes and won't hesitate to give you specific, detailed information."*
- **Insight:** the thread is 90% *detection*, 10% *what real looks like*. C.A.Gs supplies the missing affirmative half — and can literally satisfy every "real breeder" tell (specific detailed info, per-bird video, verifiable license, no payment-first pressure).
