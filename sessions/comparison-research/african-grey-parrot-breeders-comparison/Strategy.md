## The one fact both strategies are built on
The commercial breeder-selection SERP is **owned by UGC (Reddit, Facebook, forums, YouTube) + a 2010 BirdTricks listicle + thin classified directories — and, on our own name, by two automated "scam-checker" pages that call C.A.Gs "suspicious."** No breeder has published an *affirmative, documented, sourced* "how to compare African Grey breeders" resource that ranks. The entire opportunity is to fill that vacuum with primary data (our 30-breeder audit) and a documentation axis (USDA/CITES/PCR) that **every single ranking page omits.**

---

## Strategy A — "The Buyer's Bodyguard" (Scam-defense-anchored, UGC-displacement)
**Spine:** meet the fearful buyer where the SERP already is — scam fear. Lead with the despair ("it all looks like scams"), then arm them: red-flag→green-flag table, reverse-image-search, safe-payment rules, the loss + recovery path (IC3, chargeback, USDA APHIS line). C.A.Gs appears as *the breeder who passes every test.*
- **Wins:** matches ~30% scam-intent + the emotional core of the UGC; directly displaces parrotforums/BirdTricks scam content; highly shareable/linkable (rescues + forums link scam guides).
- **Risk:** if scam-heavy, it reads defensive and can feel like *every* breeder (including us) is suspect — soft on conversion, and it competes with our own `/how-to-avoid-african-grey-parrot-scams/` page (cannibalization + dup-content risk).

## Strategy B — "The Documented-Comparison Standard" (Credential-axis, affirmative-framework-anchored, dual-SERP)
**Spine:** publish the affirmative resource the SERP lacks — a tier-by-tier honest comparison (direct breeder vs classified vs marketplace vs adoption) scored on a **documentation axis** (USDA AWA verifiable, CITES paperwork, DNA cert, PBFD/APV PCR, avian-vet cert, health guarantee, safe payment, shipping). Back it with our **primary 30-breeder audit data** ("0 of 30 homepages surface a USDA license; 4 of 11 direct-breeder sites are offline"). Scam defense is *one section*, not the frame. C.A.Gs is the worked example of the standard.
- **Wins:** owns the *commercial-investigation* 55% (the bigger, higher-intent slice); the documentation axis is a moat no competitor can copy quickly; primary data earns links + AIO citations; the tier table + branded-trust schema is what outranks the scam-checkers on our own name; cleanly distinct from the scam page (comparison framing vs detection framing).
- **Risk:** more build-heavy (real tier table, sourced audit numbers, more schema); must be careful the audit data stays sourced/honest (no naming-and-shaming beyond publicly observable facts).

## Recommendation — build **Strategy B**, worn in Strategy A's opening voice **(Recommended)**
**Why (data-grounded):** (1) 55% of intent is *choose/reputable/best/near-me* (commercial-investigation) vs 30% pure scam — B targets the larger, higher-value slice while A's strength is folded in as the hook + one section. (2) The documentation axis is the **only** content dimension absent from *all* ranking pages (SERP §7, Entity-Map §7) — it's a durable moat; scam-defense content is already saturated (parrotforums, BirdTricks, trainedparrot, TikTok). (3) B keeps this page cleanly separate from `/how-to-avoid-african-grey-parrot-scams/`, avoiding the cannibalization + dup-content that a scam-first frame invites (Internal-Linking §guardrails). (4) B's tier table + real-review schema is the mechanism that pushes our owned pages above scam-detector on "is C.A.Gs legit."
**Named trade-off:** B is the heavier build (sourced audit numbers, a real 4-tier comparison table, fuller schema) and demands discipline to keep the audit data strictly to publicly observable facts. We accept the extra build cost because the moat and the branded-trust win are worth it — and we recover A's emotional pull for free by opening on the r/AfricanGrey despair.

## Open Flag — one narrow question for the breeder (Clarification Checkpoint)
**In the "Our 30-breeder audit" section (§12), do you want competitor sites named (e.g., "BirdBreeders lists prices but surfaces no USDA license or health guarantee") or kept anonymous/aggregate ("most classified aggregators…")?**
**Recommended: aggregate + name only the *tier/directory* (BirdsNow, BirdBreeders, Hoobly), never an individual small breeder.** Why: naming the large directories is fair-comment on publicly observable facts and matches how buyers search ("is birdbreeders legit"); naming a small competitor breeder invites disputes and looks like attack marketing, which undercuts the trust angle. Trade-off: aggregate framing is slightly less punchy than a named callout — we accept that for defensibility.
