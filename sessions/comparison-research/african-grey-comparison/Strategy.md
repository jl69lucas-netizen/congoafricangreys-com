## Strategy A — "The Navigation Directory" (lean router)
**Spine:** a compact hub (~3,000 words) that links the 7 spokes with a short teaser each, minimal unique content, letting the spokes do the ranking. Fast to build, clean internal-link equity.
- **Wins:** low dup-content risk (little prose to overlap), quick build, tidy link fortress.
- **Risk:** thin for a 6,500–7,000-word hub standard; **doesn't rank on the head terms itself** ("african grey comparison", "best talking parrot", "which parrot is right for me") — it just forwards traffic it never captured. Wastes the pillar opportunity in an uncontested space.

## Strategy B — "The Topical-Authority Pillar" (head-term-owning aggregator)
**Spine:** a substantial pillar that OWNS the head terms with its own assets — a **grey-vs-all-species comparison matrix**, an evidence-backed **"best talking parrot #1" claim** (4 cited authorities), a grey-centric **interactive selector**, per-rival 2–3-metric synthesis sections that tease → link each spoke, and the variant (Congo/Timneh) + gender (male/female) internal comparisons — all wrapped in **ItemList schema** signaling the aggregator role. Depth + routing + superlative + selector.
- **Wins:** captures the ~45% comparison + ~35% superlative + ~20% selector intent the fragmented competitors leave on the table; becomes the cluster's authority anchor (equity flows to spokes AND money pages); the matrix wins the AIO/featured-snippet slot; the selector matches quiz-intent nobody serves in the grey niche; uses the 7-spoke moat competitors can't replicate.
- **Risk:** biggest build in the cluster; **dup-content risk with the 7 spokes is real** — every section must SUMMARIZE + link, never copy spoke prose; requires the dup gate vs all 7 before + after.

## Recommendation — build **Strategy B** **(Recommended)**
**Why (data-grounded):** (1) the hub sits in an *uncontested* pillar-shaped vacuum (SERP §1 finding 1) — building a lean router forfeits head terms no one else owns. (2) The grey is the **#1 talking parrot on every listicle** — "best talking parrot" is a high-value term the hub can win by default with a cited claim (finding 2). (3) "which parrot is right for me" is quiz-owned; a grey-centric selector matches that intent and routes to spokes — a gap nobody in the niche fills (finding 3). (4) B monetizes the **unique 7-spoke moat** (sweep: 0 competitor hubs) as topical authority; A leaves it dormant. (5) A hub built LAST should be the keystone, not a signpost.
**Named trade-off:** B is the largest build and carries genuine dup-content risk against the 7 spokes — mitigated by the summarize-and-link discipline + `cag-duplicate-content-gate --headers` and body mode pairwise vs all 7 (before outline approval AND at final pass). We accept the build cost and the discipline because the pillar captures head terms in an uncontested space and compounds the whole cluster's authority.

## Open Flag — one narrow question for the breeder (Clarification Checkpoint)
**The interactive selector (§12): do you want it to route ONLY within African Greys (Congo vs Timneh vs male/female — keep every buyer in-brand) or also honestly route "if you want cuddles not talk, a cockatoo may suit you better" (out-to-rival, maximum trust)?**
**Recommended: honest out-to-rival routing, but always landing on a C.A.Gs spoke/verdict page** (e.g. "a cockatoo is cuddlier — here's our honest African Grey vs Cockatoo breakdown"). Why: the whole cluster's credibility is built on honesty (matches CvC/CvM/CvA), and routing a poor-fit buyer to our *comparison* (not away) keeps them on-site while earning trust that converts the good-fit buyers. Trade-off: honest routing occasionally tells a buyer a grey isn't ideal — but it lands them on our spoke, not a competitor's, so the risk is minimal and the trust gain is large.
