## 5. Authority Data Points (fetched — use for E-E-A-T + external links)
- **Contrafreeloading study (Carroll & Pepperberg 2024, via Lafeber):** individual umbrella cockatoos varied wildly — Poly 71%, Dudley 58%, Teddy Bear 25%, JJ 4%; **no significant overall difference** between greys and cockatoos, but more cockatoos exceeded 50%. Author: Irene Pepperberg, Ph.D. → grounds the "both are cognitively driven; enrichment prevents plucking" point. *Grey-vs-cockatoo-specific, unique on the SERP.*
- **Powder down / allergy (Parrot Trust Scotland + AllergyCosmos + NIH PMC9381342):** African greys AND cockatoos are BOTH high-dander "powder-down" birds (greys from Africa, cockatoos from Australasia); South American parrots (macaws) produce less. A documented case of **hypersensitivity pneumonitis from a grey parrot** exists (NIH). → **Honesty moat:** we will NOT claim the grey is allergy-safe; both are powder birds, cockatoos more so.
- **Bite force (manual research data):** Congo African Grey ≈ **61–96 N (70–220 PSI)**; large cockatoos/macaws far higher. Relatable test: a grey's bite ≈ snapping a wooden popsicle stick. → risk-mitigation section.
- **Intelligence:** Alex / Dr. Irene Pepperberg; Britannica: grey "unsurpassed as a talker." Cockatoos excel at emotional/social intelligence + tool-play (Goffin's — Auersperg studies).
