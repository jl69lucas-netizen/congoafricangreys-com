## S15 — H2: What Do the First 30 Days Look Like? (~300w) [INF timeline]

The variant you choose changes the flavor of the first month, not the structure. Teri walks every buyer through the same four phases:

### H3: The Four Adjustment Phases

**Days 1–3 — settling.** Expect quiet, low perching, and eating only when you're not staring. Congos run quieter here; Timnehs often start test-chattering by day two. Both are normal.

**Week 1 — mapping.** The bird learns the household: who moves how, what sounds mean, when food happens. Keep the routine boring on purpose.

**Week 2 — trust.** Step-ups get voluntary, treats get taken gently, and you'll hear the first experimental sounds of *your* house — a drawer, a cough, a name.

**Weeks 3–4 — stabilization.** The personality arrives. This is when Congo owners meet the watchful comedian and Timneh owners meet the easy-going one, and when we make our check-in call. Buyers of both variants get our direct line for that whole first month.

## S16 — H2: Congo vs Timneh Myths — What's Actually True? (~280w) [AI myth cards]

### H3: The Three Myths We Hear Most

**Myth: "Congos are smarter."** No evidence — both are *Psittacus* with the same celebrated cognition. Congos are more *demonstrative* about it, which reads as smarter and is really just louder scholarship.

**Myth: "Timnehs are budget greys."** The Timneh is its own recognized species (*Psittacus timneh*), IUCN-listed Vulnerable, with identical documentation requirements. It's smaller and it's priced lower here; it is not lesser.

#### H4: The Big One: "Timnehs Are Always Quieter"

The most repeated claim online, usually stated as law.

##### H5: The Reality: Individual Personality Wins

Owners of both variants report quiet Congos and loud Timnehs routinely; environment and enrichment drive noise more than variant does. Our honest version: Timnehs *trend* steadier, and a specific bird can flip the trend without apologizing.

## S17 — H2: CITES Status & Documentation (~320w) [OG doc flat-lay]

Both variants sit on CITES Appendix I — the treaty's highest protection tier, applied to African Greys at CoP17 (effective January 2017) after wild-trapping devastated wild flocks. Appendix I doesn't mean ownership is illegal; it means the paperwork proving captive-bred origin is everything.

### H3: What Paperwork Comes With Every Bird?

The full stack, both variants, no exceptions: CITES captive-bred documentation, hatch certificate with closed leg band, DNA sexing certificate, and the avian-vet health certificate. If any grey seller anywhere hesitates on any of those four, that hesitation is your answer.

#### H4: Appendix I, in Plain English

Wild-caught greys cannot legally enter the US pet trade. Captive-bred greys, documented, transfer legally within the country. The band on your bird's leg was closed when it was a chick — it physically cannot be added to a wild adult, which is why it matters more than any receipt.

##### H5: Congo (Endangered) vs Timneh (Vulnerable): The IUCN Line

The wild Congo African Grey is IUCN-listed Endangered; the Timneh, Vulnerable. Neither status touches your captive-bred bird's legality — but it's why ethical sourcing isn't a marketing word on this page. Every documented captive-bred sale removes a reason for someone to trap a wild one.

## S21 — H2: Congo vs Timneh African Grey: FAQ (10 PAA, 7C hybrid — first 3 open) (~550w)

1. **Is a Congo African Grey bigger than a Timneh?** Yes — Congos run 400–600 g with a broader build; Timnehs run 275–375 g, roughly a third smaller. Both carry the same intelligence and 40–60 year lifespan; the size difference is about presence and cage real estate, not capability.
2. **Which African Grey talks better, Congo or Timneh?** Congos are the more precise mimics with typically larger repertoires; Timnehs usually start talking earlier. If precision is the dream, Congo. If early words matter more, Timneh. Individual birds outvote both averages.
3. **Are Timneh African Greys quieter than Congos?** They trend steadier and their loud moments trend milder, but "always quieter" is a myth — enrichment and environment drive noise more than variant. Neither grey approaches cockatoo or macaw volume.
4. **Why are Timnehs cheaper than Congos?** Demand, not quality. The Congo's fame and red tail carry a premium. Our Timnehs ($1,500–$1,600) get identical documentation, screening, and hand-raising as our Congos ($1,700–$2,500).
5. **Is every "African Grey" a Congo?** In casual use, usually — which confuses buyers. "African Grey" covers two species: the Congo (*Psittacus erithacus*) and the Timneh (*Psittacus timneh*). Listings that don't say which one deserve a follow-up question.
6. **Which is better for a first-time owner?** We lean Timneh: earlier maturity (2–3 years vs 4–6), steadier temperament, and more forgiving of beginner mistakes. A well-researched first-timer can absolutely succeed with a Congo.
7. **How long do Congo and Timneh Greys live?** 40–60 years for both with proper diet, UV-B exposure, and avian-vet care. Plan for a bird that attends your retirement party.
8. **Can I keep a Congo or Timneh in an apartment?** Yes, both — with daily out-of-cage time and realistic noise expectations (dawn/dusk vocal sessions, constant ambient mimicry). Timnehs are the marginally safer pick for thin walls.
9. **When do Timnehs mature compared to Congos?** Timnehs reach behavioral maturity around 2–3 years; Congos take 4–6. Shorter adolescence means a shorter hormonal-teenager phase — the stretch where most rehoming happens.
10. **What if I pick the wrong variant?** In ten years of placing both, "wrong variant" regret is rare; "wrong expectations" regret isn't. Match the bird to your household with the scorecard above, ask us the hard questions before reserving, and either grey becomes the right one.
