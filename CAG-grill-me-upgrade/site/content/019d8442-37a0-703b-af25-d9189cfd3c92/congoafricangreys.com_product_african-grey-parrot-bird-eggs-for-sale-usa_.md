## **Gestation Period for African Grey Parrot Eggs – How Long Do African Grey Eggs Take to Hatch?**

At the heart of every African Grey parrot’s legacy lies the miracle of new life—an egg, delicately laid, carefully nurtured, and destined to become one of the most intelligent birds in the avian world. The African Grey Parrot ( [Psittacus erithacus](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Grey_parrot "Learn more about African Greys on Wikipedia")), revered for its brilliance and speech-mimicking abilities, begins its journey from a humble shell, marked by patience, devotion, and nature’s careful timing.

### **Breeding Behavior and Mating Rituals**

The mating season for African Greys doesn’t adhere to a strict calendar. Whether in the dense rainforests of Central and West Africa or within the secure confines of a professional aviary, these parrots tend to breed once or twice a year, guided more by environmental comfort and emotional readiness than by seasons.

During courtship, the male African Grey parrot performs intricate rituals—offering food to the female, bobbing his head rhythmically, and showcasing his gentle affection. These behaviors are not only vital for pair bonding but also stimulate the female’s hormonal cycle, eventually leading to egg-laying.

### **How Long Does It Take for African Grey Parrot Eggs to Hatch?**

In captive breeding programs, where conditions are meticulously controlled, the average gestation period (or more accurately, the incubation period) of an African Grey egg is 28 days. This timeframe can slightly vary based on factors like temperature, humidity, and the specific health of the breeding pair.

- 🥚 **Clutch Size:** The female typically lays 3 to 5 eggs per clutch, with each egg laid approximately 24 to 48 hours apart.

- ⏳ **Incubation Delay**: Interestingly, most hens begin incubation only after the second egg is laid. This synchronizes the hatching process, allowing the chicks to be close in age and size.

- 🐣 **Hatching Stagger**: The first two eggs tend to hatch around day 28 or 29, while the remaining eggs—carefully nestled under the mother’s feathers—may hatch two to three days later, depending on when she begins incubation.

### **Captivity vs. Wild: A Contrast in Conditions**

In the wild, African Greys nest in the hollows of tall rainforest trees, where conditions for laying eggs vary significantly. Food scarcity, predation, and environmental stress often affect egg development and hatching consistency.

- 🏞️ **Wild Incubation Range**: Wild African Grey eggs hatch within a window of 21 to 30 days, depending on the hen’s vigilance and climate conditions.

- 🌿 **Natural Clutch Size**: A wild hen typically lays 2 to 3 eggs, which increases the survival odds for each chick by reducing sibling competition.

- 🐥 **Fledging**: After hatching, baby African Greys remain in the nest for around 80 days, nurtured by both parents, before they take their first flight and begin exploring the forest canopy.


In captivity, these variables are minimized. Aviary conditions, such as stable temperatures, optimal humidity levels, and protected nest boxes, provide African Grey pairs with an environment conducive to healthy reproduction and consistent hatching success.

### **Ensuring a Successful Hatch in Captivity**

If you are incubating fertile African Grey parrot eggs outside the parent nest, you must replicate natural conditions as closely as possible.

- 🌡️ **Incubation Temperature**: For African Grey parrot eggs, the incubator should be set at approximately 99.3°F (37.4°C). Consistency is crucial, as fluctuations can adversely affect the embryo.​ **👉 [Understanding the impact of temperature on parrot embryo development](https://medium.com/@aveyincubator43/optimal-parrot-incubator-temperature-ensuring-successful-hatching-9e9f0488a14 "Choosing the right incubator for parrot egg hatching success")**

- 💧 **Humidity Levels**: Aim for a relative humidity of 35-45% during the initial incubation phases. As hatching approaches, increasing humidity slightly can assist the chick in breaking through the shell.

- 🔁 **Egg Turning**: In nature, parent birds turn their eggs to ensure even heat distribution and prevent the embryo from adhering to the shell. Replicating this, gently rotate the eggs 3-5 times daily.


Using a **digital egg incubator** with automatic turning and humidity controls can dramatically improve your hatch rate, especially if you’re a beginner or handling multiple eggs.

**❓How to tell if parrot eggs are fertile?**

Fertility can be assessed through candling. By shining a light through the egg in a dark room, you can observe developmental signs: **👉** **[Forum guide to spotting fertility in bird eggs](https://forums.avianavenue.com/index.php "How to identify fertile parrot eggs during incubation")**

- **Early Days (1-7):** Look for a network of blood vessels.​ **👉** **[Average hatch time for African Grey parrot eggs](https://pethelpful.com/ask-a-vet/when-will-bird-eggs-hatch "Understanding African Grey parrot gestation time")**

- **Mid-Incubation:** The embryo becomes more defined, and movement may be detected.​

- **Later Stages:** The egg appears darker as the chick occupies more space.​


Infertile eggs remain clear or show no signs of development.​

**❓What to do with unfertilized parrot eggs?**

✔️ If an egg is determined to be infertile:​

1. **Leave Temporarily:** Allow the bird to sit on the egg for a few days to prevent stress.​

2. **Replace or Remove:** After some time, you can replace the egg with a dummy or gently remove it.​

3. **Monitor Behavior:** Ensure the bird doesn’t exhibit distress or attempt to lay excessive replacement eggs, which can be taxing on her health.​


**❓What do you do when an African Grey lays eggs?**

✔️ If your African Grey lays eggs:​

1. **Assess Fertility:** Determine if the eggs are fertile, especially if no male is present.​

2. **Provide Calcium:** Egg-laying depletes calcium reserves. Supplementing with calcium ensures the bird’s health.​ **👉** [**Reddit insights on African Grey breeding** **issues**](https://www.reddit.com/r/AfricanGrey/comments/175qfvc/laying_eggs/ "Community discussion on African Grey egg laying")

3. **Limit Nesting Triggers:** Reduce access to dark, enclosed spaces and limit daylight hours to discourage continuous laying.​ **👉** **[Egg laying in African Greys without a mate](https://birdtricksstore.com/blogs/birdtricks-blog/single-parrots-and-egg-laying "Why single parrots may lay eggs and how to manage it")**

4. **Consult a Veterinarian:** Regular check-ups can help monitor the bird’s health during this period.​


**❓How long do African Greys sit on their eggs?**

✔️ In natural settings, African Grey females incubate their eggs for about 28-30 days, rarely leaving the nest. In captivity, if the eggs are infertile, it’s advisable to allow the bird to sit on them for a similar duration before removing them to prevent chronic egg-laying behaviors.​
