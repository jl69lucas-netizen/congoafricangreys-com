5 Critical Red Flags: How to Spot an African Grey Parrot Scam
⚠ Scam Alert 2025–26
1
💰
Price Below $1,500
Congo African Greys legitimately cost $1,500–$3,500. Any listing under $800 is a near-certain scam.
✓ Verify: Ask for CITES docs + USDA license before any deposit
2
💳
Untraceable Payment Only
Scammers demand Zelle, CashApp, Western Union, or gift cards — all non-reversible and untraceable.
✓ Safe: Credit card or PayPal Goods & Services only
3
📄
No CITES Documentation
All captive-bred African Greys require CITES Appendix II paperwork. No docs = illegal bird or fraud.
✓ Demand: Hatch certificate + band number + CITES paperwork
4
🔍
Stolen or Recycled Photos
92% of scam sites use stolen photos. Reverse image search any bird photo at images.google.com.
✓ Test: Right-click photo → "Search Image" before sending money
5
🎥
Refuses Live Video Call
A legitimate breeder will always do a live FaceTime or video call showing the actual bird in their home. Refusal = no real bird exists.
